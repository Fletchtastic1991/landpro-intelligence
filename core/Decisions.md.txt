Decision 1: LandPro is a platform, not just an app

Context: Confusion about whether LandPro was “just SitePro” or something larger
Decision: LandPro is the parent platform; SitePro is the first product built on top of it
Why: This preserves long-term flexibility and avoids locking intelligence into a single UI
Revisit?: No (unless abandoning multi-product vision)

Decision 2: LandPro Core already exists

Context: Concern about being stalled or “not far enough”
Decision: LandPro Core exists today in an early but real form
Why: Canonical data models, decision logic, workflows, and learning loops are already present
Revisit?: No

Decision 3: SitePro is a “lens” over LandPro Core

Context: Confusion around core/SiteProSection.ts
Decision: SitePro is a thin presentation layer that consumes LandPro Core; it does not define it
Why: Prevents tight coupling and allows future products to reuse the same intelligence
Revisit?: No

Decision 4: LandPro Core must not reference product-specific concepts

Context: File naming and folder structure discussion
Decision: LandPro Core cannot reference SitePro (or any future product) by name
Why: Core must remain product-agnostic to function as a true platform
Revisit?: No

Decision 5: One domain, one brand, early-stage

Context: Discussion about domains and spending money
Decision: LandPro will operate under a single domain (getlandpro.app) with subdomains or paths for products
Why: Reduces complexity, cost, and cognitive load before revenue
Revisit?: Yes (after revenue or multiple products)

Decision 6: Do not form an LLC or business entity yet

Context: Questions about when to “make it official”
Decision: Delay LLC, bank accounts, and accounting until revenue or payment intent exists
Why: Avoids premature overhead and false progress
Revisit?: Yes (triggered by first payment or contract)

Decision 7: Infrastructure is replaceable; core logic is not

Context: AWS vs “owning your own AWS”
Decision: LandPro will not build infrastructure; it will own decisions, data, and domain expertise
Why: Defensibility comes from domain intelligence, not servers
Revisit?: No

Decision 8: Preserve original ideas before refactoring

Context: Fear of forgetting earlier notes and ideas
Decision: All original thinking will be frozen in a read-only vision file before restructuring
Why: Prevents accidental loss of meaning and founder burnout
Revisit?: No
